## Brief

Tata Consumer Products, building on the success of the 'Hidden Temple' film, needed another standout commercial for Tata Gluco+ that would continue the satirical, larger-than-life campaign universe. The creative concept, developed by Wunderman Thompson, Delhi, was a sci-fi spoof: a space tour bus selling the one thing every passenger actually wants — the ride home.

The challenge was tonal. Aliens, a dead spacebus, a churn-it-back-to-life rescue — all of it primed for slapstick. The brief held the line the same way it did with the ocean film: play it straight, let the premise carry the absurdity, keep performances grounded so the comedy sits in the concept and the punchline, not in mugging for the camera.

## Our Concept & Approach

Avakkai Studio created a satirical sci-fi fantasy that follows the same universe and wink as the 'Hidden Temple' film — this time swapping Samudra Manthan for intergalactic adventure. The product does the churning, the fantasy does the selling, and the punchline ties it all back to earth.

**The Core Idea:** A satirical space-tour bus won't start. Enter the alien youth on board: they down Tata Gluco+, churn the wheels back to life, and the bus fires up into a full launch. Sci-fi spoof and product energy, fused into one fantasy.

**Production Highlights:**
- **50-Foot Space Bus Interior:** A massive cross-section of the bus interior was built practically in a Chennai studio — real scale, real mechanics, nothing faked in post
- **Motorised Miniature Rocket:** A miniature rocket was engineered to actually launch on cue, timed to the beat of the story rather than stitched together after the fact
- **Pure Craft:** No location, no stock footage, no CGI shortcuts. The entire film was a studio production from the ground up
- **Set Design Excellence:** Talent, lighting, and camera work were all choreographed around this purpose-built environment

## Outcome & Results

The campaign delivered outstanding creative impact, proving that wacky concepts deserve real production investment:

- **Practical Production Excellence:** Nothing about the spacebus was faked in post. A 50-foot cross-section of the interior was built practically, alongside a motorised miniature rocket engineered to actually launch on cue — real scale, real mechanics, timed to the beat of the story

- **Tonal Discipline:** By keeping performances grounded and letting the absurdity sit in the premise and punchline, the film avoided slapstick — landing as clever, self-aware satire

- **Campaign Universe Expansion:** The film continued the 'Gas Minus, Energy Plus' narrative thread from the 'Hidden Temple' film, creating a cohesive campaign universe with the same DNA: a story built specifically to earn its own punchline

- **Production Craft:** The film stands as proof that wacky concepts deserve real production investment — a studio production from the ground up with no compromise on quality

- **Earned the Punchline:** The entire film was built to deliver the payoff: *"This story has a lot of gas, but Tata Gluco+ is Gas Minus, Energy Plus."* The tale is deliberately, knowingly tall, so the punchline lands as a wink rather than a lecture
