## Brief

Britannia, one of India's oldest and most trusted food-products corporations, sought to capitalize on the booming mobile gaming segment to promote their Treat A Naut cookies. The objective was to create brand awareness and deep engagement among kids — their core audience — by gamifying the brand experience. As part of a larger promotional campaign, players who engaged with the game had the chance to win an all-expenses-paid trip to DisneyLand, adding a powerful incentive layer.

The challenge: create a TVC that would not just advertise the cookies, but actively introduce and build excitement around the Treat A Naut game world, driving kids to play and participate.

## Our Concept & Approach

Avakkai Studio conceptualized a space-mission adventure that brought the Treat A Naut universe to life. The creative strategy centered on a powerful insight: for kids, imagination is reality. The film positioned the game as a mission where Treat A Naut cookies serve as fuel, and adventure is the ultimate reward.

Rather than simply referencing the game, we physically recreated its world within the production design. The game mechanics — navigating spaceships, collecting treat packs, avoiding obstacles and enemy ships — were built into the set itself. This allowed young viewers to visually and emotionally step into the mission, making the story tangible and aspirational.

**Production Highlights:**
- **Location:** Mumbai
- **Duration:** Single-day shoot with full cast
- **Set Design:** Game world physically integrated into the set for immersive storytelling
- **Direction:** Every setup demanded precision, with zero room for reshoots

## Outcome & Results

The campaign delivered exceptional results across multiple fronts:

- **Seamless Brand-Game Integration:** By building the game into the production design, the film didn't just advertise — it created an entry point. Kids could see themselves in the mission, driving curiosity and engagement with the actual game.

- **Extended Campaign Lifespan:** The TVC served as the launchpad for a larger promotional ecosystem. Britannia later converted the on-screen world into a playable game, ensuring the story didn't end at 30 seconds — it gave kids a real destination.

- **Brand Awareness & Engagement:** The campaign successfully captured the attention of India's young mobile gamers, positioning Treat A Naut as an adventurous, fun-loving brand rather than just another cookie.

- **Incentive-Driven Participation:** The DisneyLand prize amplified excitement and participation, turning passive viewers into active players.

- **Production Efficiency:** Delivering a high-concept, game-integrated film within a single day in Mumbai demonstrated Avakkai Studio's ability to execute ambitious creative visions under tight constraints — with precision and flair.
