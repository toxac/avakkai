## Brief

TVS Motor Company, through a direct client engagement, needed a launch film for the Neo AMI scooter in the African market. The objective was to position the Neo AMI as a new-age, tech-loaded scooter for Africa's Gen Z riders — less a vehicle ad, more an introduction to a piece of tech that happens to ride. The challenge: create a brand look, feel, and tagline built specifically for Africa's Gen Z, not adapted from elsewhere.

## Our Concept & Approach

Avakkai Studio developed the brand identity and the film as one integrated thought from day one. The tagline, visual language, and tech-forward positioning were created together, ensuring that what comes through is a scooter built for Africa's Gen Z, styled and shot with the same energy its audience consumes content.

**The Core Idea:** Mixed media matched to the product — quick cuts, feature call-outs, and a visual language with the same energy Gen Z actually consumes content in.

**Production & Technical Approach:**
- **14-Foot LED Wall:** A large LED wall in a Bangalore studio allowed the entire environment to be under direct control, with every backdrop, light, and reflection art-directed in-camera
- **No Location Moves:** The entire shoot was completed in-studio, eliminating the unpredictability of on-location filming while delivering visually rich, varied backdrops
- **Mixed Media:** The film combined cinematic cinematography with feature-driven storytelling, highlighting the scooter's tech and design attributes
- **Direct-to-Client Collaboration:** The identity and the film were developed together, ensuring seamless alignment between brand strategy and creative execution

## Outcome & Results

The campaign delivered a launch that resonated with its target audience:

- **Originated, Not Adapted:** The brand look, feel, and tagline were built specifically for Africa's Gen Z, ensuring authentic connection with the target audience rather than repurposed global assets

- **Direct-to-Client Efficiency:** Working directly with the client meant the identity and the film weren't separate jobs — the tagline, look, and tech-forward visual treatment were developed as one thought from day one

- **In-Camera Art Direction:** The 14-foot LED wall provided complete control over environments, allowing for art-directed backdrops, lighting, and reflections without leaving Bangalore — delivering variety and quality without location logistics

- **Gen Z Visual Language:** The quick-cut, feature-forward storytelling spoke the language of its audience, positioning the Neo AMI as a tech product that happens to ride

- **Intent-Driven Execution:** The film carried the weight of a product designed for tech-savvy urban riders, establishing the Neo AMI as a serious contender in Africa's Gen Z scooter market
