## Brief

Mumbai Mirror, a leading city newspaper, sought to create a campaign that would connect younger readers to the Bombay they never saw, while honouring the city's rich history and transformation. The initiative needed to be more than just editorial content — it had to be a cultural moment that reflected on the city's evolution from Bombay to Mumbai through the eyes of those who lived it.

The objective was to pair historical BCCL archival photography with raw, unfiltered narrative reflections from five iconic citizens whose lives unfolded alongside the city's changing landscape.

## Our Concept & Approach

Avakkai Studio conceptualized and produced a five-part documentary series built on the idea that **people are the mirror images of the city they live in.** The campaign captured the parallel journeys of five senior citizens — "Bombaywallahs" who are now Mumbaikars — whose personal stories mirrored the city's own transformation.

**The Core Idea:** Reflections — both literal and metaphorical. The campaign explored how the city changed, and how its citizens changed with it, through personal narratives, historical archives, and visual symbolism.

**Featured Citizens:**
- **Julio Ribeiro** — Former police commissioner on governance and policing
- **Shirish Patel** — Urban planner and civil engineer on infrastructure and physical landscape
- **Pushpa Bhave** — Social activist and scholar on grassroots movements and the working class
- **Gerson da Cunha** — Legendary advertising guru and theatre artist on civic engagement and cultural shifts
- **J V Pawar** — Author and co-founder of the Dalit Panthers movement on social justice and identity

**Visual & Editorial Approach:**
- **Striking black-and-white portraits** combined with mirror-reflection compositions of Mumbai's historic landmarks
- **Archival integration** — the editorial and creative teams sifted through hundreds of vintage photographs from BCCL archives to support each personal narrative
- **Print innovation** — the launch-day edition featured the masthead printed in reverse, physically driving home the "reflections" theme

## Outcome & Results

The campaign delivered exceptional impact across editorial, cultural, and brand metrics:

- **Acclaimed Reception:** Mumbai Mirrored was widely praised as one of Mumbai Mirror's most distinguished branded content campaigns, resonating deeply with both older readers who remembered Bombay and younger readers discovering its history

- **Cultural Significance:** By capturing the voices of citizens who witnessed the city's transformation firsthand, the series created a permanent record of Mumbai's evolution — a gift to the city's collective memory

- **Archival Revival:** The integration of BCCL's vintage photography archives gave younger audiences a visual connection to a Bombay they never experienced, bridging generational gaps through storytelling

- **Print Innovation:** The reverse-printed masthead on launch day was a bold, talk-worthy execution that embodied the campaign's theme physically — becoming a collector's item for readers

- **Brand Positioning:** Mumbai Mirror cemented its identity as a newspaper that doesn't just report news but engages deeply with the city's soul, its citizens, and its history

- **Storytelling Excellence:** The 5-part series proved Avakkai Studio's ability to handle sensitive, culturally significant content with the same precision and creativity as commercial productions
