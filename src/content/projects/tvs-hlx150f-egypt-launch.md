## Brief

TVS Motor Company, through a direct client engagement, needed a launch film for the HLX 150F in the Egyptian market. The objective was to position the bike as a premium, feature-dense motorcycle for urban riders who demand performance and presence in equal measure. The challenge: create a brand look, feel, and tagline built specifically for urban Egypt — not localised from existing assets, but originated for the market.

The film needed to be less an introduction and more a statement of intent, carrying the weight of a product designed to compete at the premium end of the urban motorcycle segment.

## Our Concept & Approach

Avakkai Studio developed the brand identity and the film as one integrated thought from day one. The tagline, the visual language, and the premium positioning were created together, ensuring that what comes through is a bike built for urban Egypt, styled and shot with the same premium intent the product itself demands.

**The Core Idea:** Mixed media matched to a premium product — clean, feature-forward visual storytelling that lets the bike's spec sheet read like design rather than data.

**Production & Technical Approach:**
- **14-Foot LED Wall:** A large LED wall in a Bangalore studio allowed the entire environment to be under direct control, with every backdrop, light, and reflection art-directed in-camera
- **No Location Moves:** The entire shoot was completed in-studio, eliminating the unpredictability of on-location filming while delivering visually rich, varied backdrops
- **Mixed Media:** The film combined cinematic cinematography with feature-driven storytelling, highlighting the bike's design and performance attributes
- **Direct-to-Client Collaboration:** The identity and the film were developed together, ensuring seamless alignment between brand strategy and creative execution

## Outcome & Results

The campaign delivered a premium brand launch that resonated with its target market:

- **Originated, Not Localised:** The brand look, feel, and tagline were built specifically for urban Egypt, ensuring authentic connection with the target audience rather than repurposed global assets

- **Direct-to-Client Efficiency:** Working directly with the client meant the identity and the film weren't separate jobs — the tagline, look, and premium visual language were developed as one thought from day one

- **In-Camera Art Direction:** The 14-foot LED wall provided complete control over environments, allowing for art-directed backdrops, lighting, and reflections without leaving Bangalore — delivering variety and quality without location logistics

- **Premium Positioning:** The clean, feature-forward visual storytelling positioned the HLX 150F as a premium bike, with spec sheets reading like design rather than data

- **Intent-Driven Execution:** The film carried the weight of a product designed for performance and presence, establishing the HLX 150F as a serious contender in Egypt's urban motorcycle market
