## Brief

TVS Motor Company needed to celebrate the 10-year anniversary of their HLX brand in Africa, timed around the African Nations Championship — a moment when the entire continent was watching. The objective was to create a digital film that would honour the brand's decade-long presence across Africa, connect with the football fever sweeping the continent, and do so within tight constraints: no time to shoot, no live-action production.

The challenge: deliver a cinematic, continent-spanning film entirely in post-production — no unit, no location days, no live-action safety net.

## Our Concept & Approach

Avakkai Studio embraced the constraint and turned it into creative opportunity. The concept centred on a single powerful symbol: the number "10" — representing both TVS HLX's 10 years in Africa and the iconic jersey number worn by football legends across the continent.

**The Core Idea:** A "10" set loose across the continent — floating over cities, landscapes, and landmarks, travelling until it finds its final home: a jersey.

**Production & Technical Approach:**
- **3D Modelling:** The "10" was modelled in 3D, designed to feel iconic, weightless, and celebratory
- **Licensed Stock Footage:** Every frame of Africa in the film came from sourced stock footage across multiple African locations
- **Tracking & Compositing:** The "10" was tracked and composited into each shot to feel like one unbroken flight across the continent, rather than a stitched-together sequence
- **Post-Production Only:** A film with zero shoot days, built entirely at the edit and VFX desk

## Outcome & Results

The campaign demonstrated that a strong concept can survive — and thrive — without a camera on location:

- **Conceptual Strength:** The "10" worked on two levels — a decade of HLX and the football jersey number — giving the film a dual meaning it didn't need to explain

- **Post-Production Excellence:** Despite zero shoot days, the film delivered a cinematic, continent-spanning journey through precise tracking, compositing, and 3D animation

- **Perfect Timing:** The release around the African Nations Championship gave the "10" immediate cultural resonance, tying the brand's milestone to a moment the whole continent was watching

- **Efficient Production:** Proving that a strong concept doesn't need a camera on location to land; it needs the idea to be strong enough to survive being built entirely in post

- **Brand Celebration:** The film celebrated TVS HLX's decade of presence across Africa, positioning the brand as deeply connected to the continent's culture, passion, and moments of unity
