When TVS Motors came to us with briefs for Africa and Egypt, the conventional approach would have been straightforward: fly to Lagos, fly to Cairo, cast locally, shoot locally.

We had a better idea.

## The Problem with Location Shoots

International location shoots are expensive. Not just flights and accommodation — it's the logistical overhead, the time zones, the supplier network you don't know, and the quality control challenges when you're 6,000 kilometres from your core team.

For a single market, you can justify it. For five markets across two continents, you need a smarter system.

## What We Did Instead

The key insight was this: what makes a film feel *African* or *Egyptian* to its audience isn't geography. It's casting, wardrobe, cultural references, and the specific visual language of that market.

So we built that language into the production itself:

- **Casting**: we sourced talent from each target region, or talent with authentic ties to it
- **Research**: we studied the visual vocabulary of each market — what colours, settings, and scenarios resonated locally
- **Craft**: we recreated environments in India that matched the feel of each location

The result? Five campaigns that felt genuinely local to their audiences — delivered from one production base.

## The TVS Lesson

TVS trusted us with their most important emerging markets. The films ran in Africa and Egypt and were received as locally authentic content.

That's not a trick. It's production intelligence — understanding that authenticity is a craft decision, not a geography requirement.

When you work with Avakkai on international campaigns, you get the local feel without the international overhead.
