When the creative brief for Tata Gluco Jelly landed, the concept was clear: the product is bouncy, everything should bounce, the world should feel elastic and alive.

The easy answer was CGI. A competent VFX house, a few weeks in post, and you have your bouncing world.

We chose to do it live.

## Why Practical Always Wins

There's something audiences feel — even if they can't articulate it — when what they're watching is real. The weight of a body in motion. The physics of an object bouncing. The micro-expressions of talent reacting to something that's actually happening in front of them.

CGI, even excellent CGI, has a texture to it. Practical production doesn't.

## What It Cost Us

The live stunt approach meant months of pre-production we wouldn't have needed for CGI. It meant a professional stunt team, rigging, safety protocols, multiple rehearsal days, and a shoot that was genuinely logistically complex.

It was more expensive. It took longer. It was harder.

## What It Gave the Client

A film that felt real because it *was* real. A production story that became part of the brand narrative. A piece of content that audiences watched and said, "how did they do that?" — knowing the answer wasn't "computers."

That question — *how did they do that?* — is the most valuable thing a brand film can generate. It means people watched it twice.

## The Avakkai Position

We will always recommend the approach that serves the film best. Sometimes that's CGI. Often it's practical. The Gluco Jelly film taught us again what we already knew: given the choice between the easy answer and the right answer, choose right.

The stunt team was extraordinary. The client was brave enough to back us. The film was worth every minute of it.
